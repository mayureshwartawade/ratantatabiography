Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group, one of India's largest business conglomerates. He was chairman of the Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, and interim chairman from October 2016 through February 2017. He continues to head its charitable trusts.

Ratan Tata was born on December 28, 1937, in Bombay (now Mumbai), India. He is the son of Naval Tata, who was adopted by Ratanji Tata, the son of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group. Tata's parents separated when he was 10 years old, and he was raised by his grandmother, Navajbai Tata. Tata received his bachelor's degree in architecture from Cornell University in 1962. He returned to India and joined the Tata Group in the same year. He worked in various Tata companies before being appointed director-in-charge of National Radio and Electronics Company (Nelco) in 1971. Tata turned Nelco around from a loss-making company to a profitable one.

In 1991, Tata succeeded his uncle, J.R.D. Tata, as chairman of the Tata Group. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded its operations globally and acquired several major companies, including Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus. Tata also focused on developing new businesses and technologies, such as information technology and telecommunications. Tata is also a philanthropist and has donated millions of dollars to various charitable causes. He is the chairman of the Tata Trusts, which supports a wide range of initiatives in education, healthcare, and rural development.
